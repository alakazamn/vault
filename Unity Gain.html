Unity Gain

Unity gain is when input gain equals output. It is a gain factor of 1, or a 0db change.

Remember that psychologically, louder sounds better, so unity gain can help you determine the actual effect a piece of gear is having on the sound.

Further, control over the gain at each stage (gain staging) can help you to avoid unwanted build-ups in level that lead to headroom issues/distortion and signal-to-noise ratio issues:

If every piece lost or added even 1/2dB of gain, you could have signal-to-noise ratio and headroom issues; not to mention the fact the last pieces in the chain would have serious gain problems by being either over or underfed the proper level.[1]
