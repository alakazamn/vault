Bit Depth

In digital signal processing, bit depth or bit word length describes how much data is available for each sample of audio, and thus, defines the amount of dynamic range available. In the analogy to video, bit depth is like the resolution of one frame.

You can also think about bit depth as the amount of colors in your toolbox. The more colors you have, the more contrast and detail you can create in your art.

Commonly used bit depths include: 16 bit, 24 bit, & 32 bit.
