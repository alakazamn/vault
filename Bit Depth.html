Bit Depth

In signal processing, bit depth or bit word length describes how much data is available for each sample of audio, and thus, defines the amount of dynamic range available. In an analogy to video, bit depth is like the resolution of a single frame. At higher resolutions (bit depths), the picture (audio) is more detailed.[1]

Commonly used bit depths include: 16 bit, 24 bit, & 32 bit.
