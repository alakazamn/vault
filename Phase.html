Phase

In physics, phase refers to the relationship between waves in time. Two waves are in-phase if the two waves are synchronized; that is, if they are the same point in their cycle at the same time. Thus, phase shift is the displacement of a wave in time.[1] Phase is measured in degrees (°).[2]

When waves are in-phase, constructive interference occurs by the superposition principle. In other words, the waves sum to make a louder signal. Conversely, when waves are out-of-phase, destructive interference occurs. In this case, the waves cancel each other out.

Thus, it is important to consider phase between sounds, as the sounds may be unintentionally cancelling each other out or combining to increase the overall level of the signal.

a mixing tip

I generally prefer to treat drum tracks separately rather than bussing all kicks, all snares together. If it is useful for a specific reason, then summing is fine as well. If there are a number of samples, try playing with their phase instead of just trying EQ and Compression. (If I slide this kick sample 100 samples left or right does it sound better or worse?)
