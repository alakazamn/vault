Microphone

In music recording, a microphone is a transducer which converts sound energy waves into an electrical signal.[1]

Types

Dynamic

In dynamic, or moving-coil microphones, a thin coil of wire surrounds a magnet. When changes in sound pressure move the diaphragm, the attached coil of wire moves past the magnet. By the principle of electromagnetic induction, this generates an electrical signal analogous to the variations in sound pressure that moved the diaphragm.
