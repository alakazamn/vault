Sample Rate

In digital signal processing, sample rate refers to the samples per second recorded of a continuous waveform.

When signals are converted from analog to digital, discrete samples of the continuous analog signal are taken at regular intervals. Sample rate is the rate at which these samples are recorded.

In an analogy to video, sample rate is like frame rate. The size, or resolution of each sample is determined by bit depth.

According to the Nyquist-Shannon Sampling Theorem, the sample rate of a signal must be twice the highest desired frequency to avoid loss of data.
