Copyright

Copyright is a monopoly on the legal right to reproduce an original work; in other words, it’s a protection that says a certain person or group is the only party who can legally make a copy.

The length of a copyright may vary, but for works with a known author created after 1978, they last for the life of the author plus 70 years.[1]
