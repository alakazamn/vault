MS

M/S, or mid-side is a stereo micing technique. A cardoid mic captures the front, while a bidirectional mic captures the sides.

A mid-side matrix is then required to decode the signal using phase cancellations.

  • Excellent mono compatibility-- the sides cancel out, but the center remains (and is accentuated)
  • Mid to side balance can be altered after recording

[1]

Mid-Side Matrix Math

A mid-side matrix is required to decode the signal. It uses strategic phase cancellations to isolate the left and right channels.

We have both the middle channel, which is a combination of the left and right; and the side channel, which is any sound that is not in the center. In other words, the side channel whatever sound is not shared between the left and the right.

Thus, the side is equal to the left signal plus the phase-reversed right signal, so that any sound in common cancels out.

Mathematically:

Combination Result
M L+R
S L-R

Using these equations, one can then find the left channel with the information we have-- just the mid and the side:

Combination Math Result
M + S L+R+L-R 2L
M - S L+R-L+R 2R

The matrix thus must attenuate both signals to achieve the same level.

In mono, both the left and right signals combine:
(M+S) + (M-S) = 2M

As such, in mono, the center of an M/S setup is accentuated.
