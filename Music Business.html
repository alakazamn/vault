business is changing, but it's also cyclical

  • today's day-- touring / performance sells; tix crazy expensive, music basically free
    • "the making of reasonable doubt"-- was throwing parties, giving out shit to promote record
    • this was also true a while ago-- concerts were most important, records were usually sent to radio or literally given away for free
  • 1960's -- beginning of corporate mindset about art
    • "revolution will be brought to you by sony" -- at least with sony, napster, spotify breaking the industry the chokehold of giant corporations is weakened
  • 90's -- bubble
    • new laserdisk tech
      • made by sony, not in biz
      • once it took off, they took over the biz
    • problems with the medium-- had to buy all new equipment, convert entire collection
    • fragile-- rebuying same record
    • led to massive overinflation, signing artists to shelve them or to destroy competition, or just because it was less of a risk
  • napster came and blew it up
  • Now: VR on the advent-- when biz changes, look at how important stuff translates

how to learn

  • learning-- watching + copying + doing (Pedagogy)
    • you learn on the job, will figure out what's expected
    • but everything will always be changing-- people get comfortable on tape only for digital to come around, etc
    • wants us to build a framework for continued learning, understand core principles so we can pay attention to how they will be adapted to changes in biz, be flexible
      • i.e. VR is coming-- do digital concerts count as "non-interactive digital radio"
    • education doesn't engage the individual-- encouraged to view experiences as "sit here, listen to this guy talk, regurgitate, and get out"-- passive experience
    • don't learn that way-- from talking to playing an instrument
    • would like us to learn to take advantage of resources and knowledge of professors
        • don't think about stuff as "going to happen in future"-- "in school now, will do this later, etc"... should be thinking about now
    • example is that one class some kid told him all the stuff he was teaching could be found on youtube
